# Elsevier Style Guide

## Core principles

- Preserve scientific meaning.
- Prefer precise verbs over promotional adjectives.
- Make the gap, contribution, and evidence visible.
- Keep terminology consistent across title, abstract, introduction, method, experiments, and conclusion.
- Make limitations honest and proportionate.

## Avoid

- "revolutionary", "groundbreaking", "perfect", "unprecedented" unless directly justified and still likely too promotional.
- Claims of superiority without baseline names and metrics.
- Vague phrases such as "extensive experiments" without describing datasets/tasks/metrics.
- Fabricated citations or unverifiable journal rules.

## Good replacement patterns

| Weak phrase | Stronger pattern |
|---|---|
| We propose a novel method | We propose [method], which addresses [gap] by [mechanism]. |
| Extensive experiments show | Experiments on [datasets] show [specific result]. |
| The method is robust | The method remains effective under [condition], as measured by [metric]. |
| This paper has three contributions | This work contributes: (i) [method], (ii) [analysis], and (iii) [evidence]. |
