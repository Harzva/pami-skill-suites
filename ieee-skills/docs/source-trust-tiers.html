Source Trust Tiers

Publisher, journal, society, repository, template-paper, and unverified-candidate provenance levels.

Trust tiers

v1.7.0 classifies source provenance separately from live URL health. A high trust tier means better provenance, not that the cached URL was live-checked during release.

TierPriorityMeaningRecords
publisher-official10Publisher-wide official source from IEEE/Elsevier author centers, policies, templates, agreements, copyright/license/open-access pages, ethics, AI, data/code, or permissions pages.213
journal-official9Target-journal official homepage, Guide for Authors / Information for Authors, journal scope page, journal-owned submission instructions, IEEE Xplore or ScienceDirect journal page.30
society-official8Society or owner page associated with a journal, such as IEEE Computer Society or IEEE EMBS journal resources.36
repository-best-practice4Unofficial skill-suite guidance: routing policy, output contract, reviewer-risk checklist, context-safety design, and workflow recommendations.4
template-paper-observation3Structural observation from lawful paper-template corpus, official templates, or open-access papers.7
unverified-candidate1Candidate journal/source from expansion packs or incomplete metadata that lacks a full official-source matrix or live-check support.3

Offline-safe policy

Release builds remain dry-run by default. Use python scripts/live_source_check_matrix.py --live and python scripts/check_official_links_live.py --live before relying on mutable publication details.

IEEE Skills is unofficial. Verify live publisher and journal pages before real submission advice.
